# Financial Risk Management Framework for Tunicotex Group

Developed for **FIN460 — Financial Risk Management** at **Tunis Business School**.
This project presents a practical and data-driven financial risk management framework applied to **Tunicotex Group**, a Tunisian industrial firm. The objective is to identify, quantify, and manage key financial risks using standard risk metrics, stress testing, and scenario analysis under emerging market conditions.

---

## Project Overview

The project focuses on assessing the firm’s exposure to major financial risks and evaluating mitigation strategies consistent with corporate risk management best practices. Real company data and market variables are combined with quantitative risk models to support decision-making at the firm level.

---

## Key Risk Dimensions

* **Foreign Exchange Risk**: Exposure arising from import/export operations and foreign-currency-denominated transactions.
* **Interest Rate Risk**: Sensitivity of financing costs and cash flows to changes in market interest rates.
* **Liquidity Risk**: Ability of the firm to meet short-term obligations under normal and stressed conditions.

---

## Methodology

* Exposure mapping using historical cash flows and balance sheet data.
* Estimation of **Value at Risk (VaR)** using:

  * Parametric (Variance–Covariance) approach
  * Historical Simulation
* Computation of **Expected Shortfall (ES)** for tail-risk assessment.
* **Stress testing and scenario analysis** based on adverse macroeconomic and market shocks.
* Comparative analysis of **risk mitigation strategies**, including hedging and policy-level controls.

---

## Results & Insights

* Quantified downside risk across major financial exposures.
* Identified critical stress scenarios with material impact on firm liquidity and profitability.
* Demonstrated the limitations of single-metric risk assessment and the importance of tail-risk measures.
* Provided data-backed recommendations to improve financial resilience.
